Global Terrorism since 1970

Between 1970 and 2019

Global Terrorism trended upwards significantly in recent decades; In 1970 there were 65 fatal attacks worldwide, but in 2019 there were 4474. Although, only roughly half of the attacks over this period were fatal, the fatal attacks claimed more than 140,000 lives. These attacks occur across the globe in different situations and have been attributed to more than 2000 name perpetrators. Thus, this visualization will look into trends in the large variety of fatal terrorist attacks over the period 1970-2019.

Data sourced from https://www.start.umd.edu/gtd/

The Increase in Terrorism and its Regionality

Both time-series charts below show the clear increase in terrorist attacks, and conequently fatalities due to terrorist attacks, over the timeframe. However, there is variance in the prevalance of each attack type over the period, with no discernible pattern. Though terrorism is a global issue it is certainly concentrated in some regions more than others. The choropleth map suggests that India, and nearby countries, Central America, and the Middle Eastern regions have the greatest incidence of fatal terrorist attacks. The distribution of the circles representing significant terrorist attacks too suggests that some regions have a greater terrorism threat than others.

Description: The layered map has a choropleth map showing the number of fatal terrorist attacks for each country over the entire 1970-2019 period. While the circles on top show the location of terrorist attacks that had more than 100 fatalities, with the size of the circle representing the number of fatalities. The Line chart can be used to filter the attacks shown by year, and it also filters the area chart too. The legend on the area chart can be used to filter circles shown on the map by type of attack.

The Outlier Countries and Target Type

The regionality of terrorist threats becomes even more apparent when the top 30 countries with the most terrorist attacks are isolated. The majority of the countries are from the Middle East, Central America, the Indian Subcontinent or Africa. The most notable exceptions being the United Kingdom and Spain, the only countries from Eastern Europe, and even then they have comparatively few attacks when comparing against the top 10 countries. We can also show the most prevalent attack targets, by comparing them against the top 30 countries. It appears that Military, Government, Police and Private Citizens/Property are most often the target of terror attacks

Description: The heatmap shows the general trend in target type across the top 30 countries with the greatest fatal terrorist attacks, and a tooltip is available for specific details. The bar chart above shares the same country axis, and plots the total number of attacks for the respective countries over the period 1970-2019

Most Fatal Attacks, by Attack and Weapon Type

Having looked at the prevalence of attack types for all fatal attacks, we can also look at the distribution of attack types in substantial terrorist attacks (>25 fatalities and wounded). The most prevalent attack types in these substantial attacks are Bombings and Assaults, where other types have comparatively little prevalence. Furthermore, in filtering by the weapon type used in the attack we can view the relationships between weapons used and attack types . Interestingly Explosives were by far the most common category of weapon used in attacks, followed by Firearms.

Description: The chart can be filtered by weapon type by using the drop down menu, and filtered by attack type by clicking on categories in the legend. Furthermore, you can zoom and pan the chart to investigate the more dense clusters of dots in detail.

The Relationship between Attack and Target Type

We can also analyze fatal terrorist attacks by their attack type - target type relationship, to determine if there is any trends for particular combinations. The diagram only shows the significant relationships and reiterates that Assault and Bombings are the most common attack types, and shows that Private Citizens/Property is the most common target type of all. Consequently, it is unsurprising that the most common relationship is Bombings/Explosions targeting Private Citizens/Property.

Description: The Sankey chart shows all attack type - target type relationships that are significant; connected together in more than 1000 fatal terrorist attacks (between 1970-2019). The tooltip can display the exact number of connections (and the proportion of total connections) for each node and link. Additionally, by clicking on a node yo ucan view only the links in/out of that node.
